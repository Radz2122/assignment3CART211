Jim Davis

James Robert Davis is an American cartoonist, television writer, television producer, screenwriter, and film producer. He is best known as the creator of the comic strips Garfield and U.S. Acres. Published since 1978, Garfield is one of the world's most widely syndicated comic strips. Davis's other comics work includes Tumbleweeds, Gnorm Gnat, and Mr. Potato Head.
My thoughts:
I grew up reading Garfield and it was a big part of my childhood. I have read every Garfield volume at least twice because it was my favorite book to borrow from the library at the time. Even if the artl style was simple, it was clean and easily recognisable.
9/10
×

Bill Watterson

William Boyd Watterson II is a retired American cartoonist and the author of the comic strip Calvin and Hobbes, which was syndicated from 1985 to 1995. Watterson stopped drawing Calvin and Hobbes at the end of 1995 with a short statement to newspaper editors and his readers that he felt he had achieved all he could in the medium.
Calvin and Hobbes was the only english comic I read while growing up so it means a lot to me. Not only did it introduce me to the english lagnuage, it always gave me a good laugh and reading it lifted my mood. The art style is also simple and it does not have a lot of details but it's very clear.
8/10
×

Alberto Aleandro Uderzo

Alberto Aleandro Uderzo was a French comic book artist and scriptwriter. He is best known as the co-creator and illustrator of the Astérix series in collaboration with René Goscinny. He also drew other comics such as Oumpah-pah, again with Goscinny. Uderzo retired in September 2011.
Astérix was the first ever comic I've read. Every character in the comic is so unique that they're recognisable by their silhouette alone. It's very detailed and colorful which makes it a pleasant read.
8/10
×

Manfung

Manfung, also known as @l.manfung on Instagram is an artist from Hong Kong. They draw fan art but also manga-like characters of their own and sell their art online and in conventions.
Manfung is an artist I've followed for a while now. I really like their art because it's always so colorful and it inspires me in my own work. The way they shade their characters is also very unique and it's visible that they pay attention to all the little details.
8/10
×

Takehiko Inoue

Takehiko Inoue is a Japanese manga artist. He is best known for the basketball series Slam Dunk (1990–1996), and the jidaigeki manga Vagabond, which are two of the best-selling manga series in history.
The art style in these mangas is incredible. The mouvements made by the characters, jumps, leaps, fights, they all have a certain fluidity to them. In this specific manga Vagabond, his panels are very detailed and they have elegant backgrounds.
10/10
×

Tooru Fujisawa

Tooru Fujisawa is a Japanese manga artist. His name is romanized as Tohru Fujisawa on the Tokyopop English-language Great Teacher Onizuka books and as Toru Fujisawa on the Kodansha bilingual releases. His first serialized work was Adesugata Junjo Boy, published from 1989 in Weekly Shōnen Magazine. Fujisawa's best-known work is Great Teacher Onizuka (GTO) about a biker, Eikichi Onizuka, and his attempt to become and remain a teacher.
The manga seen in the picture is Great Teacher Onizuka. It's one of the first mangas I've ever read and the most marking. The art style in it is old-fashioned as it is an old book. However, the exaggerated facial expresisons on the charcaters, the backgrounds and the great attention to detail makes it a very good read.
9/10

How it started

I was first introduced to art through comics. As a kid, I went to the library every weekend to read and to borrow books. I would often borrow tons of french comics.

Manga/ Manga style artists

A few more years pass and my taste in comics changed. I started reading japanese comics, also known as mangas. The transition from full-colored comics to black and white ones was definitely a bit strange at first, but I learned to appreciate all the different art styles nevertheless. I started getting interested in art at the time and wanted to give it a go. I also started looking into artist's art instagrams.

Video Games

Games have always been a part of my life growing up and they still are. I've had a GameBoy, N64, IBM computer, Nintendo DS, PSP, PS3, PC, and Nintendo Switch. All these platforms evolved over time and so did the art in them. I started looking into games and their art in high school. I then became interested in the making of them and decided to pursue a career in the game industry.
